Some interesting things to note about our scaled down Milky Way
  • While the Milky Way is not uniformly dense, if we assume that there are 300 billion stars in the Milky Way that would average out to about one star for every 22 square meters.
  • The nearest galaxy to us, Andromida, is now 64,772 kilometers away. The Earth's actual circumference is 40,075 kilometers to put it in perspective.
