How Big Is It?

"This space stuff is just too big! I will never understand it!"

Have you heard this from someone, or even thought it yourself? Yes, space is really big and very spread out. Big things are going on and we are very small in comparison. The center of the Sun is 27,000,000 degrees Fahrenheit. Venus is 108,200,000 kilometers away from the Sun. The Supermassive Black Hole at the center of our galaxy has the mass of over 4,000,000 Suns. Ok, great. What does all that mean?

In an effort to close this mental disconnect a little lets build a pretend universe where we shrink everything down simultaneously (SCALEPERCENTAGE to be exact). At this point the Sun is about the size of a hot air balloon. Now use the address bar below (if you like) and move your new hot air balloon sun to an address where you are familiar with the surroundings. From there you can check out the other objects to get a better feel for where things are in our Solar System.

You can move the Sun with this. You can also drag the Sun marker as well.

The speed of light is incredibly... slow?

If you walk into a dark room and flip on the lights it is illuminated, to our eyes, instantly. We have never had to wait on light. However, light does have a speed limit. And when you are talking about the great void of space we should start thinking about light and the objects we see around us a little differently. If we scaled down the speed of light by the same amount we have scaled everything else then the speed of light is only going about SCALEDSPEED which is the speed of a fast jog.
